Trump's Trade War: Tariffs & Global Impact

How protectionist policies reshaped international trade relations and what it means for the future of globalization

By Shourya Jain Published: 15 Apirl,2025

Introduction

A tariff is a duty (a tax) imposed by a national government, customs territory, or supranational union on imports (or, exceptionally, exports) of goods. Besides being a source of revenue, import duties can also be a form of regulation of foreign trade and policy that burden foreign products to encourage or safeguard domestic industry When Donald Trump became the U.S. President in 2017, slowly and gradually his Administration started reshaping the American trade policy under the banner of "America First." The goal was to correct trade imbalances and protect U.S. industries, The strategies implemented, incured aggressive tariffs which became tension for global economy.

When Donald Trump assumed the U.S. presidency in 2017, his administration gradually reshaped American trade policy under the "America First" banner. This initiative aimed to rectify trade imbalances and shield U.S. industries from foreign competition. The implementation of aggressive tariff policies created significant tensions in the global economy.

The Strategy Behind Trump's Trade Wars

The Trump administration's trade war stemmed from the belief that previous U.S. trade agreements - particularly those with China, Mexico, Canada, and the European Union - were fundamentally unfair. In response, the government imposed tariffs on steel, aluminum, washing machines, solar panels, and thousands of Chinese goods to counteract perceived unfair trade practices.

Reduce Trade Deficits

Bring down the gap between imports and exports to strengthen the domestic economy and reduce foreign dependence.

Revive U.S. Manufacturing

Protect and create jobs in key industries by making imported goods more expensive than domestic products.

Fair Trade Deals

Renegotiate existing trade agreements to secure better terms for American workers and businesses.

Major Tariff Actions Timeline

  • January 2018: 30% tariffs on solar panels and washing machines
  • March 2018: 25% tariffs on steel and 10% on aluminum imports
  • July 2018: First round of tariffs on $34 billion of Chinese goods
  • September 2018: Tariffs expanded to $200 billion of Chinese imports
  • June 2019: Mexico tariff threat over immigration issues

Economic Impacts on the United States

The global reaction to this was very quick, Targeted countries responded with changes in their own tariffs on American products, instead of making it as a global political issue. This caused the U.S economy in many ways:-

Higher Costs for Businesses and Consumers

As tariff increased the prices of aluminium solar panels etc increased which became the reason of increased prices for both business and consumers.

Agricultural Sector Struggles

Farmers faced devastating losses as China slashed purchases of U.S. agricultural products, requiring billions in government aid.

Impact on Household Budgets

The rates of necessary products increased so the average household budget increased. In U.S an average household budget is approximately $800 which got increased to $1000 per year.

Global Repercussions

The ripple effects of Trump's tariff policies extended beyond U.S. borders, significantly impacting global markets:

New Trade Alliances Formed

  • RCEP: World's largest trade bloc covering 30% of global GDP
  • EU-Japan Trade Agreement: Created to counter protectionist trends
  • African Continental Free Trade Area: Accelerated regional integration
Supply Chain Chaos

Multinational company faced increased costs as tariff made the supply routes more expensive and less reliable .

Shifts in Global Trade Alliances

Due to these tariffs global economies had made new trade partnership such as Regional Comprehensive Economical Partnership(RCEP) which excluded U.S.A from it.

Emergence of Economic Nationalism

Other countries are trying to adopt the similar policy changes, either inspired or pressurized by U.S economy to resha the global market..

Business started following 'china plus one starergy' reducing the dependency of china and by increasing alternative options in countries like Mexico, India, Vietnam.

Strained U.S-China relation

The trade war made the economic competition between the U.S. and China even stronger. Even after Trump left office, most tariffs on Chinese products stayed in place

Pressure on Global Trade Institutions

There is a high pressure on the World Trade Organization (WTO) who is having hard time to solve this fight between 2 big economies of the world and trying to fix this issue as soon as possible.

Looking Ahead: The Future of Global Trade

The trade conflicts marked a pivotal shift in international commerce, signaling the end of the relatively open global trading system that characterized the early 21st century. The emerging paradigm appears to emphasize:

Key Takeaways

  • Tariffs were used as both economic tools and political statements
  • The trade war accelerated deglobalization trends already underway
  • Supply chains became more regional and resilient
  • Technological competition intensified between US and China
  • The WTO's role in trade disputes became less relevant

Conclusion

Trump's trade wars marked a significant shift in international trade and went beyond simple disputes over tariffs. They exposed the flaws in how countries depend on each other for products, spurred more countries to focus on defending their own economy, and developed a new, more guarded and divided trade system. The impact of his tariffs on international trade, politics, and future decisions is evident, whether of whether you believe they improved or worsened the situation. For many years to come, the globe will have to cope with the changes these policies brought about.
